The Water Cycle Process

The water cycle has several stages:

  • Evaporation: Water from oceans, lakes, and rivers is heated by the sun, turning into water vapor.
  • Condensation: As the water vapor rises and cools, it condenses into clouds.
  • Precipitation: When clouds become saturated, water falls to the earth as rain, snow, or hail.
  • Collection: The water collects in bodies of water, like rivers, lakes, and oceans, ready to be evaporated again.

Why is the Water Cycle Important?

The water cycle is crucial for many reasons:

  • It ensures a continuous supply of water for ecosystems.
  • It regulates weather patterns and contributes to rainfall.
  • It is essential for growing food and maintaining agriculture.
  • The water cycle helps distribute heat across the planet, affecting temperatures and climates.
